I am an Associate Professor in the <a href="https://liberalarts.temple.edu/academics/departments-and-programs/political-science">Department of Political Science</a> at <a href="http://www.temple.edu/">Temple University</a>. Previously, I was an Assistant Professor in the <a href="http://www.memphis.edu/polisci/">Department of Political Science</a> at the <a href="http://www.memphis.edu/">University of Memphis</a>, and before that I was a postdoctoral scholar at the <a href="http://www.vanderbilt.edu/csdi/">Center for the Study of Democratic Institutions</a> at <a href="http://www.vanderbilt.edu/">Vanderbilt University</a>. I received my PhD in <a href="http://web.mit.edu/polisci/">Political Science</a> from the <a href="http://web.mit.edu/">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a>.

My research seeks to deepen our understanding of American democracy, primarily through an examination of state and local governments. To do so, I combine experimental and quasi-experimental designs with novel, rich data sets built from from surveys, election results, and administrative archives. Learn more about my research <a href="/research/">here</a>.
