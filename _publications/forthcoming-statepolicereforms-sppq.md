The 2020 Black Lives Matter protests dramatically increased the salience of police reform, yet the US Congress failed to pass any reforms. In contrast, state governments have passed hundreds of police-related bills since 2020. I summarize the plethora of state reforms passed over this period by grouping them into 18 key areas, including 14 I classify as pro-reform and 4 I classify as anti-reform. I find that state party control is a robust predictor of enacting pro-police reform policies, and that reforms are more likely in states with more liberal and more pro-reform voters. While police reforms are responsive to public opinion, they are also typically incongruent.
